# AI Education Creator Strategy
## Modeled on Mariah Brunner (@itsmariahbrunner / learnaiwithmariah.com) — adapted into your own brand

> **How this research was done:** Instagram and TikTok block direct viewing without a login, so this analysis is built from her website (which mirrors her content — nearly every free guide on it is the "comment X and I'll send it" companion to one of her videos), her public media kit, and web/search data about her account and videos. Her guide library is effectively a public record of her best-performing topics, which is exactly what you asked for.

---

## Part 1 — Who she is and why her account "pops"

### The numbers (as of mid-2026)
- **160K–173K followers**, grown in **under 4 months**
- **11M+ monthly views** combined across Instagram + TikTok
- **~16.5% engagement rate — 10x the industry average**; 1M+ interactions/month on Instagram alone
- Audience: majority **female, 25–54, professional, non-technical** — people who want to use AI at work, not build it

### Her positioning (this is the single most important thing to copy structurally)
Her tagline is **"Master AI Before Monday."**

She is **not** positioned as a developer, engineer, or AI researcher. Her credibility stack is:

1. **Corporate proof:** ex-Amazon, ex-Meta ("I learned how world-class companies operate")
2. **Business proof:** built two 8-figure e-commerce businesses
3. **The hook that ties it together:** *"AI now runs ~90% of my operations."*

So her authority isn't "I understand transformers." It's **"I use this every day to make real money, and I'll show you exactly how."** That's why non-technical professionals trust her — she's one of them, just further ahead.

### Why the account grows so fast — the mechanics

**1. She rode a specific wave instead of covering "AI" generally.**
She went nearly all-in on **Claude** (Projects, Skills, connectors, scheduled tasks, Claude Code, Cowork) at the exact moment interest exploded and almost every other creator was still making generic ChatGPT prompt content. Owning a specific, rising, under-covered tool made her *the* person for it. This is the biggest lesson: **pick a lane with rising demand and low creator supply.**

**2. Every video is a "watch me actually do it" build, not a talking-head opinion.**
Screen recording + voiceover/face bubble, showing a real workflow producing a real result (a workout plan connected to Apple Health, a wedding planner, an interview coach, an ad-writing system). Viewers save and share videos that feel like *usable instructions*, and saves/shares are what the algorithm rewards.

**3. The "comment a keyword" engagement engine.**
Nearly every video ends with: *"Comment 'METHOD' and I'll send you the full breakdown."* (via ManyChat-style DM automation). This does three things at once:
- Floods the post with comments → algorithm boosts it
- Moves the viewer into her DMs → she sends a link to a guide on **her website**
- The guide page captures the **email** → she now owns the audience

**4. Content and product are the same thing.**
Each video → becomes a free guide on her site → free guides funnel into paid offers. Zero wasted work. Her site's guide list *is* her greatest-hits reel.

**5. Specificity in every title.**
Never "How to use AI better." Always: *"Cut Claude tokens by 75%,"* *"10 secret words that make Claude 10x better,"* *"5 Claude skills every real estate agent needs."* Numbers, named tools, named professions, named outcomes.

## Part 3 — Your version: the male-perspective AI educator

### Don't copy her lane — mirror her *structure* with a different audience skew
Her audience is majority women 25–54 in professional roles. The equivalent underserved male-skewing audiences:

- **Trades & blue-collar business owners** (contractors, HVAC, landscaping, auto) — almost nobody teaches them AI, and they run on quotes, invoices, scheduling, and lead follow-up that AI crushes
- **Sales guys & agency owners** — outreach, follow-up, proposals, CRM hygiene
- **Gym/fitness, sports, and money/investing angles** for life-content crossover (her equivalents were weddings and language learning)
- **9–5 corporate men who want a side income** — the "escape the cubicle with AI" story

**Recommended positioning:** *"Regular guy who uses AI to run the boring 90% of his work — and shows you exactly how, step by step."* Pick a tagline with the same energy as "Master AI Before Monday" — e.g. "Let AI do your busywork," "Work like it's 2030," or "Your unfair advantage at work."

### Your credibility stack (fill in your real story)
You don't need Amazon/Meta on your resume. You need: **(1) a relatable "before,"** (2) **a specific result AI got you** (hours saved per week, revenue, a system that runs itself), and (3) **receipts on screen** — show the actual dashboards, calendars, and outputs. If you don't have the result yet, your story is the build-in-public version: *"I'm automating my entire job/business with AI in 90 days — follow along."* That's a proven growth format too.

### Content formula (per video)
1. **Hook in the first 2 seconds**, spoken + on-screen text: a number, a named tool, a named outcome. ("Claude has a hidden feature that plans your whole week — here's the 60-second setup.")
2. **Screen recording of the real build**, talking over it, 30–90 seconds, fast cuts, show the finished output first when possible ("look at what this made — here's how").
3. **CTA:** "Comment '[KEYWORD]' and I'll send you the exact prompt/skill."
4. Every video becomes a **guide page on your site** with an email gate.

## Part 4 — Building your website (your learnaiwithmariah.com)

### What to put on it
1. **Home** — tagline, 2-sentence story, photo, three buttons: Free Guides / Flagship Program / Newsletter
2. **/guides/** — start with 5–10 guides (one per video you post). Each guide page: the video embedded, the copy-paste prompt/skill, an email gate ("get the full version + weekly drops")
3. **Newsletter** — weekly "here's what changed in AI + one thing to build this week"
4. **Paid offer, phase 2** (after ~10–20K followers): a **"One-Weekend AI Setup"** low-ticket product ($27–97), then a **30-day challenge/cohort** ($200–500) once you have an email list
5. **/media-kit/** — publish your stats early, even when small; niche B2B/AI sponsors pay well for engaged professional audiences
6. **/contact/**

### How to build it (cheap → done this week)
- **Domain:** yourname.com or a brandable ("learnaiwith[name].com" is too close — pick your own pattern, e.g. "aidoneby[name].com" / "[name]automates.com")
- **Site:** Carrd (fastest, ~$19/yr), Framer, or Squarespace (what her site appears to be). Don't over-engineer — hers is a simple content site.
- **Email:** ConvertKit (Kit) or Beehiiv — both have free tiers and landing pages
- **DM automation:** ManyChat (the "comment X → auto-DM the link" engine — this is load-bearing in her system, ~$15/mo)
- **Later:** Stripe/Gumroad or Skool/Circle for the paid community when you launch the challenge

### 90-day launch plan
- **Days 1–7:** Lock positioning + name, film 5 videos before posting anything, set up Carrd + Kit + ManyChat, write 3 guides
- **Days 8–30:** Post 1x/day on TikTok + IG (same video), every video keyword-gated to a guide, start weekly newsletter. Goal: find the 2–3 formats that hit for *you* — double down on them
- **Days 31–60:** 1–2x/day, launch the profession series ("5 AI skills every ___ needs"), publish media-kit page, collab/duet with adjacent creators
- **Days 61–90:** Launch the low-ticket "One-Weekend Setup" to your email list, tease the 30-day challenge, pitch 2–3 sponsors using your stats

### The three rules that make her system work (don't skip these)
1. **One tool-depth lane, not "AI news."** Own a specific rising tool/workflow the way she owned Claude.
2. **Every video ships a usable asset.** If a viewer can't copy-paste something afterward, it's not a video yet.
3. **Video → guide → email → offer.** Never post content that doesn't feed the funnel.
