25 Claude Prompts Worth Stealing 🎁

From @techyai_cj. I write code for a living and use Claude every single day. These are the prompts I actually reuse. Tap copy, paste into Claude, swap the brackets for your stuff.

⚡ Work faster

the everyday time savers
01

Read this document and give me: the 3 things I should worry about, the 3 deadlines or numbers that matter, and anything unusual compared to a standard version of this kind of document. [paste or attach]

02

Here is my messy to do list. Rewrite it as a plan for today in time blocks. Protect 2 hours of deep work, batch the small stuff, and tell me what to drop entirely. [paste list]

03

Summarize this email thread in 5 bullets: what happened, who wants what, what I actually need to do, by when, and a draft reply in my voice. [paste thread]

04

Turn these rough notes into a clean one page brief with sections for goal, context, decisions needed, and next steps. Keep my wording where it works. [paste notes]

05

Build me a spreadsheet that tracks [thing]. Include the columns a professional would use, 3 example rows, and one formula that tells me the number I should check weekly.

✍️ Write better

sound like you, not like AI
06

Here are 3 things I wrote. Learn my voice from them. From now on in this chat, write everything the way I would. First task: [task]. [paste samples]

07

Rewrite this so a smart 15 year old gets it on the first read. Keep every fact. Cut every word that is not pulling weight. [paste text]

08

Now make it better. Cut anything generic, sharpen the first sentence, and end on the strongest point. Show me what you changed and why.

09

Write 5 completely different opening lines for this. One curiosity, one bold claim, one number, one question, one story. [paste draft or topic]

10

Be my tough editor. Read this and tell me: where you got bored, what you did not believe, and what you would delete. Be specific, not polite. [paste draft]

💰 Money and business

for side hustles and small businesses
11

You are a pricing analyst. Here is what I sell and what I charge. Tell me if I am underpricing, what the market range looks like, and give me 3 ways to raise prices without losing customers. [describe]

12

Draft a professional estimate for this job: [describe job, your rates, materials]. Line items, subtotal, terms, and a friendly closing note. Make it look like it came from an established company.

13

Write a friendly check in text to past customers asking how things are running and mentioning I appreciate referrals. Three versions: casual, professional, and short.

14

Here are my monthly subscriptions and roughly what I pay. Which would you cancel first and why? What is the total I would save per year? [paste list]

15

I have [X hours] a week and this skill: [skill]. Give me 3 realistic ways to make an extra $500 a month with it. No dropshipping, no courses, nothing that needs an audience. Then break down week one for the best option.

🧠 Learn anything

Claude as your personal tutor
16

Explain [topic] in 5 levels: like I am 10, like I am 16, like a college student, like a professional, like an expert. Stop after each level and ask if I want the next one.

17

I want to learn [skill] in 30 days with [X minutes] a day. Build me the plan: what to do each week, what to practice daily, and how I will know I am actually improving.

18

Quiz me on [topic]. One question at a time, wait for my answer, tell me what I got wrong and why, and get harder as I get better.

19

I am about to make this decision: [describe]. Argue against it as hard as you can. Then argue for it. Then tell me what a wise friend who knows me would say.

20

Interview me for this exact role. One question at a time, follow up on weak answers, and grade me honestly at the end with what to fix. [paste job posting]

🔥 The power moves

the ones people do not know about
21

Based on everything in this conversation, what are 5 things you could do for me that I probably do not know you can do?

22

Ask me 5 questions about my goals for the next year. Then write me a letter from myself 5 years from now, specific to my answers.

23

Act as a psychologist. Based on how I have communicated in this chat, tell me the patterns you see in how I think, what probably drives me, and one blind spot I have. Be specific, not flattering.

24

Here is my resume and a job posting. Tell me what a recruiter would reject me for in the first 10 seconds, then rewrite the top third of the resume to fix it. [paste both]

25

Build me a simple working web app that does [thing]. Dark theme, clean design, works on my phone. Explain what you built in 2 sentences when you are done.
